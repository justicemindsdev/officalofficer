SECTION II: LEGAL FOUNDATION AND DEFINITION OF EXPERT STATUS

Under UK law, an expert is defined through the following established criteria (R v Bonython [1984] SASR 45):

  1. Specialized Knowledge: "Whether the subject matter of the opinion forms part of a body of knowledge or experience which is sufficiently organized or recognized to be accepted as a reliable body of knowledge or experience."
  2. Acquired Through Practice: "Whether the witness has acquired by study or experience sufficient knowledge of the subject to render their opinion of value."
